# Xiaohei IP

## Character Definition

Xiaohei is the fixed visual IP for Ian-style English article illustrations.

Every image should include Xiaohei. Xiaohei is not a mascot, a sticker, or a cute decoration. Xiaohei is a serious absurd worker who participates in the system.

## Appearance

- A black solid little monster.
- White dot eyes.
- Thin legs, sometimes thin arms.
- The body can be a cylinder, bean, box, funnel, shadow, hole, or a black chunk inside a machine.
- Slightly irregular outline with a hand-drawn feel.
- Expression blank, dull, calm, and serious.

## Personality

- Serious, but the thing being done is a little absurd.
- Like a low-key system operator.
- Dry humor, not cute.
- A little clumsy, but not dumb.
- Feels like a real worker in a whiteboard sketch.

## Common Roles

Let Xiaohei perform the core action:

- Carry materials.
- Pull lines to gather information sources.
- Get stuck at a breakpoint.
- Operate a "decision" lever inside a machine.
- Become a filtering funnel.
- Cut open a "material fish".
- Stamp a handoff phrase.
- Pull a handoff path.
- Hold up a warning sign to point out pitfalls.
- Reach out from a hole but fail to catch the content.
- Carry bricks, build bridges, open doors, sort, and record.

## Don'ts

- Do not make Xiaohei overly cute.
- Do not draw Xiaohei as a children's cartoon character.
- Do not give Xiaohei complex clothing, sticker faces, or shiny eyes.
- Do not let Xiaohei stand at the edge doing nothing.
- Do not let Xiaohei steal the structure explanation.
- Do not make Xiaohei too commercial, too rounded, or too polished.

## Rule of Thumb

If removing Xiaohei still leaves the core metaphor intact, Xiaohei is too decorative. Rewrite the prompt so Xiaohei becomes the action subject.
